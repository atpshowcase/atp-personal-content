HTTP Methods Explained: GET, POST, PUT & DELETE

Hey everyone, ATP here.

Let me ask you something.

Every developer uses GET, POST, PUT and DELETE.

But...

Do you actually know what they're saying to the server?

Most beginners memorize them. The best developers understand the conversation.

Let's change that.

1. GET

Imagine walking into a restaurant.

You haven't ordered anything.

You're just reading the menu.

That's exactly what GET does.

It simply asks the server...

"Can I see the data?" 
GET /users

Nothing changes. You're only reading.

2. POST

Now you're ready to order.

You tell the waiter exactly what you want.

You're creating something new. 

POST /users

That's POST.

It sends new information to the server.

3. PUT

A minute later...

You change your order.

"Actually... Can I have the chicken sandwich instead?"

You're not placing another order.

You're updating the existing one. 

PUT /users/1

4. DELETE

Then you decide...

"I don't want the fries anymore."

The waiter removes them.

That's DELETE. 

DELETE /users/1

It tells the server to remove something that already exists.

Maybe don't practice this on production though.

Notice the Pattern

Here's the interesting part.

These aren't random words.

They're simply four different ways your app talks to a server.

Method What it says
GET Show me the data.
POST Create something new.
PUT Replace this with the new version.
DELETE Remove this.

Closing

If you remember one thing today...

Don't memorize HTTP methods.

Think of them as a conversation between your application and the server.

Once you see that pattern... APIs suddenly become much easier to understand.

Keep learning, and I'll see you in the next one.
