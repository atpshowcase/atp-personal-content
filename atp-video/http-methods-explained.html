🎥 HTTP Methods Explained (60-90 seconds)

Opening

Hey everyone, ATP here.

Let me ask you something.

Every developer has seen GET, POST, PUT, and DELETE.

But...

Do you actually know what they're telling the server to do?

If not, don't worry.

By the end of this video, you'll never mix them up again.

GET

Imagine you're at a restaurant.

You open the menu.

You look around.

You haven't ordered anything yet.

You're just checking what's available.

That's exactly what GET does.

It asks the server,

"Can I see the data?"

Nothing changes.

You're only reading.

GET /users

POST

Now you're ready to order.

You tell the waiter,

"I'd like a cheeseburger."

Something new is being created.

That's POST.

You're sending new information to the server.

POST /users

PUT

Then the waiter comes back.

You change your mind.

"Actually... make that a chicken burger."

You're replacing the whole order.

That's PUT.

It completely overwrites the existing data.

PUT /users/123

DELETE

But then you realize you left your wallet at home.

You panic.

"Cancel everything!"

That's DELETE.

It wipes the record out of existence.

So remember:

GET to read.

POST to create.

PUT to replace.

DELETE to destroy.

DELETE /users/123
